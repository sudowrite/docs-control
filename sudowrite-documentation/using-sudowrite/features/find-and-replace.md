Find and Replace lets you search for and replace text across your project — whether you need to rename a character, fix a recurring typo, or update a setting name across dozens of chapters and Story Bible entries.

## How to Open Find and Replace

There are two ways to open Find and Replace:

-   **Magnifying glass icon** — Click the 🔍 icon at the top left of the editor.
    
-   **Keyboard shortcut** — Press **Cmd + F** (Mac) or **Ctrl + F** (Windows/Linux). If that shortcut triggers your browser's built-in search, press it a second time to access Sudowrite's Find and Replace instead.
    

## Choosing a Scope

Find and Replace includes a **scope dropdown** that lets you control where your search runs. There are three scopes:

### Project-Wide

Searches across **every document _and_ your entire Story Bible** in the current project. This is the most powerful scope — ideal for renaming a character or setting that appears throughout your manuscript. The tool shows how many matches were found across all documents, with a clear breakdown of where each reference appears.

### Story Bible Only

Searches **exclusively within your Story Bible** — including Characters, Worldbuilding, Synopsis, Outline, Style, and Genre fields. Use this when you need to update Story Bible entries without touching your chapter prose.

### Current Document

Searches **only within the document you currently have open** in the editor. Best for quick, targeted edits within a single chapter or section.

## Using Find and Replace

1.  Open Find and Replace using the magnifying glass icon or keyboard shortcut.
    
2.  Type your search term in the **Find** field. Matches will be highlighted in your text, and you'll see a count of how many were found.
    
3.  By default you’ll be presented Project-Wide search results, but you can use the **scope dropdown** to target your Story Bible or Current Document exclusively.
    
4.  To replace matches, type your replacement text in the **Replace** field.
    
5.  Click **Replace** to replace the currently highlighted match, or **Replace All** to replace every match within your selected scope at once.
    

## Tips

-   **Renaming characters:** Use the Project-Wide scope with Replace All to rename a character across your entire manuscript and Story Bible in one step.
    
-   **Be specific:** If your search term is short or common (like a character named "Al"), review matches before using Replace All to avoid unintended replacements.
    
-   **Large projects:** Project-wide searches across very large projects with many documents or extensive Story Bibles may take a moment to complete. The tool will still work — it just needs a bit more time to scan everything.
